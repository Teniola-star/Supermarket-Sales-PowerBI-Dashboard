# Supermarket Sales Performance Dashboard (Power BI)

## Project Overview

This project is a Power BI dashboard built to analyze supermarket sales data and provide insights into customer behavior, product performance, and overall business trends.

The main objective of this project is to transform raw transactional data into meaningful business insights that can support data-driven decision-making.

---

## Problem Statement

The supermarket data was analyzed to answer key business questions such as:

- Which product lines generate the highest sales?
- Which city performs best in terms of revenue?
- What payment methods are most preferred by customers?
- Which customer type contributes the most revenue?
- What overall trends can be observed in sales performance?

---

## Tools and Technologies Used

- Power BI Desktop
- Data Visualization
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- Data Cleaning and Transformation (Power Query)

---

## Key Metrics

- Total Sales: 322.97K  
- Gross Income: 15.38K  
- Average Sales: 322.97  

---

## Key Insights

### 1. City Performance
Naypyitaw recorded the highest sales among all cities.  
This may be influenced by higher customer activity, stronger demand for products, or better purchasing behavior in that location compared to other cities.

---

### 2. Product Line Performance
Food and Beverages emerged as the best-performing product line.  
This is likely because it is a high-demand essential category with frequent customer purchases compared to other product categories.

---

### 3. Payment Method Analysis
E-wallet was the most used payment method.  
This suggests a strong preference for digital payment systems, likely due to convenience, speed, and possible promotional incentives.

---

### 4. Customer Type Analysis
Members generated more revenue compared to normal customers.  
This indicates that loyalty programs and membership benefits may significantly influence customer purchasing behavior and retention.

The dashboard includes interactive visuals that allow users to explore the data effectively:
